### Separating Axis Theorem - SAT
> If you can find any axis where the projections of two shapes don't overlap, they
aren't colliding.

1. For each edge of each polygon, compute the normal (perpendicular) to that edge — this is your "separating axis."
2. Project all vertices of both shapes onto that axis.
3. If the projected ranges don't overlap on any axis, the shapes are not colliding.
4. If they overlap on all axes, the shapes are colliding.
